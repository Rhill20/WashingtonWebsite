Welcome to Washington State

Washington State is known as the "Evergreen State". Washington has a wide variety of nature and urban life. Washington offers many parks and nature reserves, some examples are Mount St. Helens, Snoqualmie Falls, Mount Rainier National Park, Olympic National Park, and many more. Washington also has a rich urban culture. In Seattle you can visit the space needle, visit the Pike Place Market, or go to Capitol Hill. There are countless cities and nature parks to explore.

Facts about Washington State

  • The population of Washington is 7.71 million
  • The Capitol of Washington is Olympia. Click here to view more about Olympia.
  • Washington was established in 1889
  • Washington has over 1,000 historic places, and 17 national natural landmarks
  • Washington produces more apples than any other state
  • Seattle was the first city to have police on bicycles
  • The Lunar Rover that was used on the moon was made in Seattle by Boeing
  • There is over 35,000 miles of streams and river
  • There is over 7,000 lakes
